Summary

What is TogoMCP?

TogoMCP is an advanced Model Context Protocol (MCP) server developed by the Database Center for Life Science (DBCLS) that revolutionizes how AI assistants interact with biological databases. By integrating over 20 major biomedical databases into a unified interface, TogoMCP enables researchers to perform complex cross-database queries, data integration, and knowledge discovery through natural language conversations with AI assistants like Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini CLI.

How It Helps Your Research

TogoMCP eliminates the need to manually query multiple databases, learn complex query languages, or write custom scripts for data integration. Instead, you can simply describe your research question to an AI assistant, which uses TogoMCP to:

  • Find proteins associated with diseases and their drug targets
  • Analyze enzyme distributions and biochemical pathways
  • Compare chemical diversity between natural products and synthetic compounds
  • Discover structural and functional relationships across biological entities
  • Generate comprehensive reports with cross-referenced data from multiple databases

Usage Examples

Below are real-world examples demonstrating TogoMCP's capabilities for complex biological data analysis:

Example 1: Cardiovascular Disease Drug Target Analysis

Prompt

"Find proteins that are associated with both cardiovascular diseases and have known small molecule inhibitors in ChEMBL, and classify them according to disease and drug availability."

Response

The AI assistant used TogoMCP to perform a comprehensive multi-database analysis, integrating data from MeSH, ChEMBL, and UniProt. The process involved:

  • Identifying cardiovascular disease terms in MeSH ontology
  • Searching ChEMBL for cardiovascular drug targets with known inhibitors
  • Querying protein structural and functional data across multiple databases
  • Cross-referencing molecular targets with clinical development phases
  • Organizing findings by disease category and drug approval status
Tools Used
  • list_databases - Identified available RDF databases
  • search_mesh_entity - Found cardiovascular disease terms
  • search_chembl_target - Located cardiovascular drug targets
  • get_MIE_file - Retrieved database schema and structure
  • run_sparql - Executed multiple SPARQL queries across ChEMBL
Key Results

The analysis identified 75+ human proteins with cardiovascular disease associations and known inhibitors, organized by:

  • Disease Categories: Hypertension (15+ targets), Coronary Artery Disease (20+ targets), Heart Failure (12+ targets), Cardiac Arrhythmias (10+ targets)
  • Target Families: Ion channels (60+ subtypes), Beta-adrenergic receptors (25+ inhibitors), ACE/AT1 receptors (17 inhibitors each)
  • Drug Status: 50+ approved drugs, 15+ Phase 3 trials, validation of key therapeutic pathways

Example 2: Human Enzyme Classification Analysis

Prompt

"Determine the distribution of human enzymes by their EC number classes."

Response

The AI assistant leveraged TogoMCP to query UniProt's Swiss-Prot database for comprehensive enzyme classification data. The workflow included:

  • Retrieving MIE file to understand UniProt RDF structure and enzyme annotations
  • Constructing SPARQL queries to extract human enzyme data with EC numbers
  • Aggregating enzyme counts by EC number class
  • Calculating distribution percentages across all seven EC classes
  • Generating visualization and detailed statistical analysis
Tools Used
  • get_MIE_file - Retrieved UniProt database schema
  • run_sparql - Executed queries to extract and aggregate enzyme data
Key Results

Analysis of 4,442 unique human enzymes revealed the following distribution:

  • EC 2 (Transferases): 1,823 proteins (41.0%) - Most abundant class, including kinases crucial for cell signaling
  • EC 3 (Hydrolases): 1,655 proteins (37.3%) - Second most abundant, including proteases and lipases
  • EC 1 (Oxidoreductases): 545 proteins (12.3%) - Important for redox reactions
  • EC 4-7 (Lyases, Isomerases, Ligases, Translocases): Combined 526 proteins (11.8%) - Specialized metabolic functions
  • Key Insight: Transferases and hydrolases dominate (78.3% combined), reflecting the importance of cellular signaling and regulatory processes

Example 3: Natural Products vs Synthetic Compounds Analysis

Prompt

"Use ChEBI (chemical classification) + ChEMBL (bioactivity) + PubChem (chemical descriptors) to compare the bioactivity profiles and chemical diversity of natural products versus synthetic compounds across different therapeutic areas."

Response

The AI assistant conducted an extensive comparative analysis integrating three major chemical databases through TogoMCP. The sophisticated workflow involved:

  • Retrieving MIE files for ChEBI, ChEMBL, and PubChem to understand their RDF structures
  • Identifying natural products and synthetic compounds using ChEBI chemical classification
  • Querying ChEMBL for bioactivity data (IC50 values, target specificity)
  • Extracting molecular descriptors from PubChem (molecular weight, chiral centers, sp3 fraction)
  • Analyzing polypharmacology patterns and therapeutic area coverage
  • Synthesizing findings into comprehensive comparative report
Tools Used
  • get_MIE_file - Retrieved schemas for ChEBI, ChEMBL, and PubChem
  • run_sparql - Executed complex queries across multiple databases
  • get_pubchem_compound_id - Retrieved PubChem identifiers
  • get_compound_attributes_from_pubchem - Extracted molecular descriptors
Key Results

The analysis revealed fundamental differences between natural products and synthetic compounds:

  • Natural Products: Ultra-high potency (70% with IC50 < 100 nM), complex structures (avg 8 chiral centers, MW 400-900 Da), polypharmacology (8.5 targets avg), dominate oncology (~60% of anticancer drugs), higher sp3 fraction (0.6)
  • Synthetic Compounds: Selective potency (40% with IC50 < 100 nM), simpler structures (avg 1 chiral center, MW 300-600 Da), monopharmacology (2-3 targets), dominate cardiovascular/CNS/metabolic diseases, lower sp3 fraction (0.3)
  • Strategic Insight: Natural products occupy unique chemical space with pre-validated biological activity, while synthetic drugs excel in target selectivity. Optimal strategy combines both approaches - using natural products for novel scaffold discovery followed by medicinal chemistry optimization

Setup Guide

TogoMCP can be easily integrated with various AI assistants. Follow the setup instructions for your preferred platform:

Claude Desktop

Connect TogoMCP to Claude Desktop for seamless access to life sciences databases. There are two methods for connecting remote MCP servers.

Method 1: Settings UI (Recommended for Paid Plans)

Requirements: Claude Desktop with Pro, Max, Team, or Enterprise plan

  1. Open Claude Desktop
  2. Navigate to Settings ‚Üí Connectors
  3. Click "Add custom connector" at the bottom
  4. Paste the TogoMCP server URL: https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp
  5. Click "Add" to save

Method 2: JSON Configuration (Alternative)

This method uses npx mcp-remote to connect to remote servers via JSON configuration.

  1. Open Claude Desktop and go to Settings ‚Üí Developer ‚Üí Edit Config
  2. This opens claude_desktop_config.json
  3. Add the TogoMCP configuration:
{
  "mcpServers": {
    "togomcp": {
      "command": "npx",
      "args": [
        "mcp-remote",
        "https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp"
      ]
    }
  }
}
  1. Save the file and restart Claude Desktop
  2. The TogoMCP tools will appear in your conversations

ChatGPT

Connect TogoMCP to ChatGPT to leverage life sciences databases in your conversations and deep research workflows.

Requirements: ChatGPT Plus, Pro, Business, or Enterprise plan

  1. Open ChatGPT and navigate to Settings
  2. Go to Connectors in the settings menu
  3. Click Advanced Settings
  4. Enable "Developer Mode (beta)"
  5. Click Create next to "Browser connectors"
  6. In the "New Connector" modal, fill in:
    • Name: TogoMCP
    • Description: Access to 20+ life sciences databases via SPARQL
    • MCP Server URL: https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp
    • Authentication: None (or OAuth if required)
  7. Check "I trust this application" and click Create
  8. The TogoMCP tools will now be available in your conversations

Note: Custom connectors are not verified by OpenAI. Only add connectors from trusted sources.

Gemini CLI

Configure Gemini CLI to use TogoMCP for command-line access to life sciences databases.

  1. Install Gemini CLI if you haven't already
  2. Locate your Gemini CLI configuration directory
  3. Create or edit the settings.json file
  4. Add the TogoMCP configuration. Important: TogoMCP uses Streamable-HTTP, not SSE:
{
  "mcpServers": {
    "togomcp": {
      "httpUrl": "https://togomcp.rdfportal.org/mcp"
    }
  }
}
  1. Save the configuration file
  2. Restart Gemini CLI
  3. TogoMCP tools will now be available in your CLI sessions

Available Databases

TogoMCP provides access to 20+ major life sciences databases in RDF format. Each database is carefully curated and maintained to ensure data quality and interoperability.

UniProt

Comprehensive protein sequence and functional information with 444M proteins. Includes Swiss-Prot (manually curated, 923K entries) and TrEMBL (automatically annotated). Features sequences, functions, domains, structures, variants, and cross-references to 200+ databases.

ChEMBL

Manually curated bioactive molecules database with 2.4M+ compounds, 1.6M assays, and 20M bioactivity measurements. Enables queries for compound-target-activity relationships, drug mechanisms, and repositioning opportunities.

PubChem

Comprehensive chemical database with 119M compounds, molecular descriptors (SMILES, InChI), 339M substances, 1.7M bioassays, and integrated chemical ontologies. Links compounds to genes, proteins, pathways, and diseases.

PDB (Protein Data Bank)

3D structural data for 204K+ proteins, nucleic acids, and complexes from X-ray, NMR, and cryo-EM. Includes experimental methods, resolution, sequences, and cross-references to UniProt and EMDB.

Ensembl

Comprehensive genomics database with genome annotations for 100+ species. Contains genes, transcripts, proteins, and exons with genomic locations. Cross-referenced to UniProt, HGNC, NCBI Gene, Reactome, and OMIM.

ChEBI

Chemical entities ontology with 217,000+ small molecules, atoms, ions, and functional groups. Provides molecular data (formula, mass, InChI, SMILES), biological roles, and hierarchical classification with cross-references to 20+ databases.

Reactome

Pathway database with 22,000+ pathways across 30+ species. Includes molecular interactions, biochemical reactions, protein complexes, and disease associations. Based on BioPAX Level 3 ontology.

Gene Ontology (GO)

Controlled vocabulary with 48,165 terms across biological processes (30,804), molecular functions (12,793), and cellular components (4,568). Standardizes gene product annotation across all organisms.

MeSH

Medical Subject Headings from the National Library of Medicine. Hierarchical vocabulary thesaurus with descriptors, qualifiers, and supplementary records across 16 main biomedical categories.

MONDO

Comprehensive disease ontology integrating multiple sources (OMIM, Orphanet, DOID, MESH, ICD) for clinical research and precision medicine. Provides unified disease classification.

Rhea

Expert-curated database of 17,078 biochemical reactions with 11,763 compounds. All reactions are atom-balanced with cross-references to metabolic pathways (KEGG, MetaCyc, Reactome) and enzyme classifications.

NCBI Gene

Gene database with 57M+ entries covering protein-coding genes, ncRNAs, and pseudogenes. Includes gene symbols, chromosomal locations, and cross-references to Ensembl, HGNC, and OMIM.

ClinVar

Genomic variation database with 3.5M+ variant records and clinical interpretations. Aggregates relationships between genetic variations and human health conditions.

PubMed

Biomedical literature database with millions of citations from MEDLINE and life science journals. Includes publication metadata, abstracts, authors, and MeSH term annotations.

MedGen

NCBI's medical genetics portal with 233,000+ clinical concepts. Integrates data from OMIM, Orphanet, HPO, and MONDO for clinical genetics research and variant interpretation.

NCBI Taxonomy

Comprehensive biological taxonomy covering 3M+ organisms with hierarchical relationships, scientific and common names. Essential for biological data integration across species.

DDBJ

DNA Data Bank of Japan with nucleotide sequence data from INSDC. Contains genomic annotations (genes, CDS, tRNA, rRNA) with taxonomic classification and functional annotations.

GlyCosmos

Comprehensive glycoscience portal integrating glycan structures (GlyTouCan), glycoproteins, glycosylation sites, glycogenes, and lectin-glycan interactions across 100+ named graphs.

BacDive

Bacterial diversity metadatabase with 97,000+ strain records. Contains standardized information on taxonomy, morphology, physiology, cultivation conditions, and molecular data for bacteria and archaea.

MediaDive

Microbial culture media database with 3,289 standardized recipes from DSMZ. Includes 1,489 ingredients with chemical cross-references and 45,685 strain records with growth conditions.

NANDO

Japanese intractable disease ontology with 2,777 disease classes. Includes multilingual labels (English, Japanese) and cross-references to international disease ontologies (MONDO).

PubTator Central

Biomedical entity annotations from PubMed literature using text mining and manual curation. Contains disease and gene annotations with MeSH and NCBI Gene identifiers.

Available Tools

TogoMCP provides a comprehensive set of tools for interacting with biological databases. These tools enable everything from database exploration to complex SPARQL queries and ID conversion.

TogoMCP Usage Guide

TogoMCP_Usage_Guide()

Provides comprehensive guidance on using the RDF Portal, including MIE file analysis, RDF exploration, SPARQL query construction, and TogoID integration. Essential starting point for understanding the workflow.

List Databases

list_databases()

Returns a complete list of available RDF databases with descriptions. Use this to discover which databases are accessible and understand their scope before starting queries.

Get MIE File

get_MIE_file(dbname)

Retrieves the Metadata Interoperability Exchange (MIE) file for a specific database. Contains ShEx schema, RDF examples, and SPARQL query templates essential for understanding the database structure before querying.

Get SPARQL Example

get_sparql_example(dbname)

Retrieves example SPARQL queries for a specific database. Provides templates and patterns for common query types to help users get started quickly.

Run SPARQL

run_sparql(dbname, sparql_query)

Executes SPARQL queries on a specified RDF database. The core tool for retrieving biological data through semantic queries. Returns results in JSON format.

Get Graph List

get_graph_list(dbname)

Returns a list of named graphs available in a specific RDF database. Useful for understanding the organizational structure and data partitioning within databases.

TogoID Convert ID

togoid_convertId(ids, route, limit, offset)

Converts identifiers from one database to another using TogoID. Supports direct and multi-hop conversions across 105+ datasets. Specify conversion route as comma-separated dataset names.

Search UniProt Entity

search_uniprot_entity(query, limit)

Searches for UniProt protein entities by query. Returns protein identifiers matching the search criteria. Useful for finding proteins by name, function, or other attributes.

Search ChEMBL Molecule

search_chembl_molecule(query, limit)

Searches for chemical molecules in ChEMBL database by query. Returns compound identifiers and basic information.

Search ChEMBL Target

search_chembl_target(query, limit)

Searches for drug targets in ChEMBL by query. Returns target identifiers useful for drug discovery and pharmacology research.

Search PDB Entity

search_pdb_entity(db, query, limit)

Searches PDB, Chemical Component Dictionary, or BIRD databases for structural biology entries. Specify database type (pdb, cc, or prd) and search keywords.

Search MeSH Entity

search_mesh_entity(query, limit)

Searches for Medical Subject Headings (MeSH) terms by query. Returns MeSH descriptors for standardized biomedical terminology.

Search Reactome Entity

search_reactome_entity(query, rows, species, types)

Searches the Reactome knowledgebase using keyword search for pathways, reactions, and complexes. Supports filtering by species and entity types.

Search Rhea Entity

search_rhea_entity(query, limit)

Searches Rhea database for biochemical reactions using keyword search. Returns reaction identifiers and equations.

Get PubChem Compound ID

get_pubchem_compound_id(compound_name)

Retrieves PubChem compound identifier for a given compound name. Essential for accessing PubChem data programmatically.

Get Compound Attributes from PubChem

get_compound_attributes_from_pubchem(pubchem_compound_id)

Retrieves comprehensive compound attributes from PubChem RDF including molecular descriptors, chemical properties, and cross-references.

NCBI E-utilities Tools

Multiple tools for searching NCBI databases including PubMed, Gene, Taxonomy, ClinVar, MedGen, and PubChem via E-utilities:

  • ncbi_search_pubmed_entity(query, max_results, sort_by)
  • ncbi_search_ncbigene_entity(query, max_results, organism)
  • ncbi_search_taxonomy_entity(query, max_results)
  • ncbi_search_clinvar_entity(query, max_results)
  • ncbi_search_medgen_entity(query, max_results)
  • ncbi_search_pubchem_compound_entity(query, max_results)

Related Resources

Explore additional resources that complement TogoMCP for comprehensive life sciences data integration:

RDF Portal

https://rdfportal.org

A comprehensive repository of life science datasets in RDF format developed by DBCLS and NBDC. The RDF Portal provides downloadable datasets and SPARQL endpoints for direct querying. Features over 40 RDF datasets, 858 million inter-dataset links, and 160 billion triples, enabling integrative utilization of heterogeneous biological data. The portal ensures data quality through rigorous review processes and maintains consistency across databases through standardized RDF formats.

TogoID

https://togoid.dbcls.jp

An exploratory ID converter service developed by DBCLS that bridges biological datasets through identifier conversion. TogoID currently covers 105+ life science databases across diverse biological categories including genes, proteins, compounds, diseases, and genetic polymorphisms. The service supports both direct and multi-hop ID conversions, enables exploration of biological relationships through semantic annotations, and provides both a web interface and API access. TogoID's unique feature is its ability to interpret biological meaning while converting IDs through the TogoID Ontology, distinguishing relationships such as "glycans bind to proteins" or "glycans are processed by proteins."

DBCLS (Database Center for Life Science)

https://dbcls.rois.ac.jp

The Database Center for Life Science is a Japanese research institute and part of the Research Organization of Information and Systems (ROIS), founded in 2007. DBCLS conducts comprehensive research in database integration technologies, from fundamental Semantic Web and RDF development to user-friendly web services and tools. The center develops technologies that enhance database interoperability, maintains the RDF Portal, organizes the annual BioHackathon series since 2008, and works collaboratively with research institutes worldwide to advance integrated life sciences databases. DBCLS plays a crucial role in the Japanese bioinformatics community and contributes to international data sharing initiatives.
